## Before Starting a Project

### Define Clear Objectives

Successful projects start with clear goals:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify business objectives">
    Ask yourself:
    - What business problem are we solving?
    - What does success look like?
    - How will we measure success?
    - What's the expected ROI?
    
    **Example:** Instead of "We need a website," try "We need a website that generates 50 qualified leads per month for our consulting services."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Know your audience">
    Understand who will use your solution:
    - Demographics (age, location, profession)
    - Technical proficiency
    - Devices they use (desktop, mobile, tablet)
    - Pain points and needs
    - Preferred communication channels
    
    <Tip>
      Create user personas to help everyone on the project understand your target audience.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Research competitors">
    Study what others in your space are doing:
    - What features do they offer?
    - What works well in their solutions?
    - What could be improved?
    - How can you differentiate?
    
    **Don't copy, but learn from** what's already been proven in your market.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## During the Project

### Communication Best Practices

<Accordion title="Be Responsive">
  Quick responses keep projects on track:
  
  **Recommended response times:**
  - Urgent approvals: Same day
  - Design reviews: 2-3 business days
  - Content feedback: 3-5 business days
  - General questions: Within 24 hours
  
  **What happens with delays:**
  - Project timeline extends
  - Team may move to other projects
  - You may lose your scheduled launch slot
  - Rush fees may apply to recover timeline
  
  <Tip>
    Set calendar reminders for expected deliverables so you can plan time for thorough reviews.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Consolidate Feedback">
  Provide comprehensive feedback in single reviews:
  
  **Do this:**
  - Review the entire deliverable thoroughly
  - List all feedback in one message
  - Prioritize items (must-have vs. nice-to-have)
  - Include screenshots or annotations
  
  **Don't do this:**
  - Send feedback as you think of items over several days
  - Have multiple stakeholders send separate feedback
  - Change your mind after we've implemented previous feedback
  - Provide vague or conflicting directions
  
  **Template for good feedback:**
  ```
  Design Review - Homepage V2
  
  MUST FIX:
  1. Logo is too small - increase by 50%
  2. Contact button text should be "Get Quote" not "Contact"
  3. Hero image doesn't represent our services - let's use [alternative]
  
  NICE TO HAVE:
  1. Could we try a darker blue? (#1E40AF instead of #3B82F6)
  2. Testimonials section could be more prominent
  
  QUESTIONS:
  1. Can the contact form capture custom fields?
  2. Will the mobile menu include social icons?
  ```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Include Relevant Stakeholders Early">
  Avoid late-stage surprises:
  
  **Who should be involved:**
  - Decision makers (final approval authority)
  - End users (will use the solution daily)
  - Technical team (if integrating with existing systems)
  - Marketing team (for messaging and positioning)
  - Legal/compliance (for regulated industries)
  
  **When to involve them:**
  - Kickoff meeting: All key stakeholders
  - Design reviews: Decision makers + end users
  - Technical reviews: Technical team + decision makers
  - Final approval: All stakeholders sign off
  
  <Warning>
    Bringing in new stakeholders late in the project often leads to major change requests and delays. Identify everyone upfront.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

### Managing Scope and Changes

<Accordion title="Understand What's In Scope">
  Know exactly what's included in your project:
  
  **Review the proposal carefully:**
  - What pages/screens are included?
  - How many revision rounds?
  - What integrations are covered?
  - What happens after launch?
  
  **Ask before assuming:**
  - "Is X feature included?"
  - "Can we add Y without impacting timeline?"
  - "What would it cost to include Z?"
  
  <Note>
    If something isn't explicitly listed in the proposal, it's probably not included. Always ask for clarification.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling Scope Creep">
  Scope creep is when projects grow beyond original plans:
  
  **How it happens:**
  - "While we're at it, could we also add...?"
  - "This should be quick, can we just..."
  - "I just realized we also need..."
  
  **How to manage it:**
  1. **Acknowledge the request** - We understand the need
  2. **Assess the impact** - Time and cost implications
  3. **Provide options:**
     - Add to current project (timeline/budget increase)
     - Include in Phase 2 (launch now, enhance later)
     - Quick workaround now, proper solution later
  4. **Get formal approval** - Change order signed
  
  **Prevention tips:**
  - Be thorough in initial requirements
  - Prioritize features (must-have vs. nice-to-have)
  - Plan for a Phase 2 from the start
  - Trust that MVP can be enhanced later
</Accordion>

## Security Best Practices

### Account Security

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use strong, unique passwords">
    **Password requirements:**
    - Minimum 12 characters (longer is better)
    - Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
    - No dictionary words or personal information
    - Unique to Tykode (don't reuse from other sites)
    
    <Tip>
      Use a password manager like 1Password, LastPass, or Bitwarden to generate and store complex passwords securely.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Secure your email account">
    Since your email is your account recovery method:
    - Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
    - Use a strong password
    - Review authorized devices regularly
    - Be wary of phishing attempts
  </Step>

  <Step title="Be cautious with sharing credentials">
    **Never share:**
    - Passwords via email or chat
    - Login credentials on unsecured connections
    - Access with untrusted third parties
    
    **Safe sharing methods:**
    - Use password managers with secure sharing
    - Create separate user accounts for team members
    - Use role-based access (minimum necessary permissions)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Project Security

<Accordion title="Protecting Sensitive Information">
  **What to protect:**
  - Customer data and personal information
  - Payment information (never share card numbers)
  - Business intelligence and trade secrets
  - API keys and integration credentials
  - Unpublished content or products
  
  **How we protect it:**
  - Encrypted connections (HTTPS, SSL)
  - Secure file transfer protocols
  - Access controls and permissions
  - Regular security audits
  - NDA agreements for sensitive projects
  
  <Warning>
    If your project involves sensitive data (health, financial, personal), inform us early so we can implement appropriate security measures.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Domain and Hosting Security">
  **Best practices:**
  - Enable domain locking (prevent unauthorized transfers)
  - Use strong passwords for registrar accounts
  - Enable 2FA on hosting and domain accounts
  - Keep contact information current
  - Monitor domain expiration dates
  - Use privacy protection on WHOIS (if appropriate)
  
  **What we do:**
  - Implement SSL certificates (HTTPS)
  - Configure firewalls and security rules
  - Regular security updates and patches
  - Backup systems and disaster recovery
  - Monitoring for suspicious activity
</Accordion>

### Launch Day

<Steps>
  <Step title="Schedule strategically">
    **Best launch times:**
    - Tuesday-Thursday (avoid Monday chaos, Friday departures)
    - Morning (allows full day for monitoring)
    - Avoid holidays or major events
    - Consider your audience's timezone
    
    <Tip>
      Launch when key team members are available to respond to any issues.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor closely">
    **Watch for:**
    - Server errors or downtime
    - Broken functionality
    - Performance issues
    - User complaints or confusion
    - Analytics anomalies
    
    **We monitor** the first 24-48 hours intensively and provide a post-launch report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Have a rollback plan">
    If something goes wrong:
    - We can quickly revert to the previous version
    - Address critical issues immediately
    - Schedule a fix for non-critical items
    - Communicate transparently with users
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

<Warning>
  Learn from others' mistakes!
</Warning>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Underestimating content preparation">
    **The mistake:**
    Thinking content can be created quickly during the project.
    
    **The reality:**
    Good content takes time to research, write, edit, and approve.
    
    **The solution:**
    Start content creation before the project begins. Hire a professional copywriter if needed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Designing by committee">
    **The mistake:**
    Including too many people in design decisions, leading to conflicting feedback.
    
    **The reality:**
    Design by committee often results in bland, compromised solutions that satisfy no one.
    
    **The solution:**
    Designate a single decision-maker or small group (2-3 people max) for design approvals.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Feature bloat">
    **The mistake:**
    Trying to include every possible feature in the first version.
    
    **The reality:**
    More features = longer timeline, higher cost, more complexity, harder to use.
    
    **The solution:**
    Launch with core features (MVP), then add based on real user needs and feedback.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Ignoring mobile users">
    **The mistake:**
    Focusing only on desktop experience.
    
    **The reality:**
    50-70% of web traffic is mobile. Mobile-first is essential.
    
    **The solution:**
    Review and test mobile experience thoroughly. Sometimes mobile UX should drive desktop design, not vice versa.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Skipping user testing">
    **The mistake:**
    Assuming you know what users want without testing.
    
    **The reality:**
    What seems intuitive to you may confuse users. Your assumptions are often wrong.
    
    **The solution:**
    Test with real users (not just your team) before launch. Even 5 user tests reveal major issues.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Neglecting SEO">
    **The mistake:**
    Treating SEO as an afterthought or add-on.
    
    **The reality:**
    SEO foundations must be built from the start. Retrofitting is harder and less effective.
    
    **The solution:**
    Include SEO in the initial project scope. Plan content and site structure with SEO in mind.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="No maintenance plan">
    **The mistake:**
    Thinking "launch and forget."
    
    **The reality:**
    Websites and apps require ongoing updates, security patches, and optimization.
    
    **The solution:**
    Budget for monthly maintenance or learn to do it yourself. Neglected sites become security risks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
